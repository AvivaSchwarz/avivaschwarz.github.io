About

I am a senior at the Bronx High School of Science in New York City. I am passionate about STEM, particularly engineering with a focus on environmental applications and mitigating anthropological climate change.

At school, I am an engineer on the all-girls robotics team (FRC 2265), and I mentor several middle school robotics teams (FLL). I am also the Chairwoman of the NYC FIRST Student Event Coalition (SEC).

I currently am working with Dr. Joaquim Goes's lab at the Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory, part of Columbia University, and we are studying algal blooms of the Arabian Sea. We are in the progress of writing and publishing two papers together.

Programming Projects

Student Overflow

My first hackathon project, Student Overflow, is an application designed by students, for students. Student Overflow optimizes the forum- based question and answer uses of yahoo answers and reddit, but without the unecessary mess. We used Material Design principles to create an intuitive user interface, and we provided the features that we know students search for when they look for academic help online.

Vendaje

Our app targets children and teaches children how to self-treat minor injuries such as bug bites, cuts, and bee stings. If the child is injured and not near a parent or guardian, they can follow the steps to perform first aid on minor injuries. We made the app as usable for very young children as possible, and all treatments can be played out loud in English or Spanish so children with limited reading proficiency will be able to follow the intuitive design. Another major feature of our app is the emergency button which calls 911.
